The History of Care Packages

Care packages started in post-World War II Europe, where these packages that supply essential things to survive were sent to soldiers and others far from home. These packages were first sent by CARE or Cooperative for American Remittances to Europe. Families relied on CARE packages that consist of mainly dry food items that were sent every four months to fill their stomachs. The CARE packages are a symbol of American generosity as a means of diplomacy to rekindle their relationship with Europe and as a mission to help people in need. According to CARE's president, Michelle Nunn. "The idea that you could send support to strangers on the other side of the world — that was a novel idea."

The care packages were first packed with essential food such as rice, beans, powdered eggs and milk. Later on, they have adjusted to Asian palette ingredients and kosher foods that was sent out to Jewish refugees. Today, CARE has expanded to Northern Europe, Asia, and Africa.

CARE has now switched over from boxes to electronic banking systems as donations. They provide families with a debit card to be able to buy what they need in their circumstances. This also helps in saving costs in transportation as well as helping their local business and farmers. Currently, CARE is focused on ending child malnutrition.

CARE’s Gifts with Lasting Change

A gift with a lasting change is a gift that empowers everyone around the world. These are gifts that you can share joy and is well appreciated when received. A gift that makes a lasting change is a great way to recognize friends or loved ones on whatever occasions. CARE is an organization that helps create solutions to poverty around the world. CARE’s goal is to provide perfect gift options for various interests or budgets in return it helps fund CARE in helping people around the world in 94 countries.

Surveys on American’s Love for Care Packages

Based on the surveys done by CARE, 92 percent of Americans wish to receive a care package and 98 percent wish to send one. This indicates that Americans still have the nature of helping someone in need whether they are their family, friends or refugees around the world according to CARE’s CEO, Michelle Nunn. The world now is experiencing a humanitarian crisis from conflicts in Syria and Yemen, hunger in Ethiopia, Somalia and other African countries. In the survey, 76 percent of Americans interpret the meaning of “care package” as “basic necessities for someone in need” and 58 percent interpret it as “something that shows you care”. In addition, Millenials or people ages 18-34 said they would most likely send care packages to a refugee. This means that working people of today would take an effort to spend their time and efforts to contribute to causes such as sending out care packages or other help to people in need around the world.

New Examples for Sharing Gifts, Trips, and Products

The Spark by Genius Series highlights three social apps that help share products.

> Social Gift
Social Gift helps with group gift-giving by saving archived content for an event. It helps spread the word for a planned collective gift through polls by allowing them to vote for which gift to get and a collective online transaction.
> Capsule
Capsule creates a platform for planning events that include a chat and share feature for easy communication. It allows users to document and capture their experience to save as memories or to just keep track for future use.
> Thinng
Thinng is a platform that is similar to Pinterest but is focused on saving products as pins. The app is based in Australia. This product is used to easily gather and compare a product choice.
